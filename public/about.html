My Journey

Hi, I'm Long. I'm a Teaching Assistant, Computer Science graduate, and MBA holder who's passionate about making technology more human and learning more meaningful. Fluent in both English and Vietnamese, I've always been shaped by international environments, from growing up in global classrooms to now working with diverse teams at the very university I graduated from - British University Vietnam, where I support students both academically and personally.

My journey began with a deep curiosity. Not just for computers and data, but for people, stories, and self-growth. Along the way, I've worked on a large-scale enterprise software project, that experience taught me how to balance detail-oriented work with big-picture thinking, something I carry into everything I do.

My experience as a Teaching Assistant at the British University Vietnam isn't just a job, it's a passion. I've had the chance to lead business-related events, organise hackathons, support Experience Days, and facilitate academic showcases and workshops.

Beyond the Code

Outside of work, I've built and founded a football club with my old friends that's now a beloved community space among Hanoi's international school alumni. I also find meaning in journaling, creating content, and mentoring others - always looking for ways to bridge who I am with what I can give.

I don't know everything — in fact, I believe there's still so much for me to learn. But I care deeply, learn constantly, and do my best to make things clearer, kinder, and more connected — all while continuing to explore how people grow, create, and connect.
