MBA Thesis: Entrepreneurship Education and Student Intentions at British University Vietnam

A study exploring how entrepreneurship education activities influence students’ entrepreneurial intentions, with a focus on psychological and educational factors within the BUV context.

MBA Thesis Entrepreneurship Education SEM ANOVA Student Intentions Higher Education
Research Type
MBA Thesis
Institution
British University Vietnam
Method
SEM and ANOVA
Focus
Entrepreneurship education and student intentions

Research Problem

Entrepreneurship is increasingly important in modern economies, but there is still a need to better understand how entrepreneurship education actually shapes students’ intentions and mindset. This study focused on the BUV context to examine how educational activities influence entrepreneurial intention.

Methodology

The research used quantitative analysis to examine relationships between entrepreneurship education and entrepreneurial intention. Structural Equation Modeling and ANOVA were used to assess both direct and indirect effects, including the role of psychological traits such as entrepreneurial self-efficacy, perceived behavioral control, and attitude toward entrepreneurship.

Key Findings

The study found that entrepreneurship education activities positively influence entrepreneurial intentions. It also showed that psychological factors play an important mediating role, and that business students tended to demonstrate stronger entrepreneurial intentions than non-business students.

Why It Matters

This research contributes to a better understanding of how universities can foster entrepreneurial thinking more effectively. It also provides practical recommendations for improving curriculum design, mentorship, and extracurricular support in higher education.
